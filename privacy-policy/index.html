Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 17, 2026

Fuse Team ("Fuse Team," "we," "us," or "our"), a sole proprietorship operated by Boris Furman and based in the United States, respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect information when you interact with our timesheet management services, including our SMS text messaging program.

Fuse Team operates a business-to-business (B2B) software service. Our direct customers are businesses ("Clients"), and the individuals who interact with our platform are typically employees or contractors of those Clients ("End Users").

1. Information We Collect

  • Client account information: Business name, address, administrator names, email addresses, and billing information.
  • End User information: Name, mobile phone number, email address, and employment identifier, provided to us by the Client through their onboarding or HR process.
  • Timesheet data: Hours worked, dates, project or task codes, and related timekeeping information submitted by End Users via SMS or web interface.
  • Message logs: Records of SMS messages sent and received through the service, retained for operational, support, and compliance purposes.
  • Technical information: Log data, IP addresses, device identifiers, and usage metrics related to our website and platform.

2. How We Use Information

We use collected information to:

  • Deliver timesheet reminders and late-submission notices to End Users.
  • Receive, process, and store timesheet data submitted via SMS or web.
  • Provide the service to Clients and respond to support requests (HELP keyword, email, web).
  • Maintain, secure, and improve our platform.
  • Comply with legal, tax, and contractual obligations.

3. SMS Messaging Program

No sharing of mobile information for marketing. Mobile phone numbers and SMS opt-in consent will not be shared with any third parties or affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes. Mobile information is shared only with subcontractors that support the operation of the messaging service (such as Twilio) and mobile carriers required to deliver messages.

End Users are enrolled by their employer (our Client), who is responsible for obtaining any necessary consent from the End User to provide their phone number to us. End Users activate the SMS program by texting the keyword START to our designated Twilio number, at which point they receive a confirmation message.

Message frequency varies based on timesheet deadlines (typically 1–5 messages per week). Message and data rates may apply. End Users may opt out at any time by replying STOP, which immediately removes them from all SMS communications. Reply HELP for assistance, or contact us at the address below.

4. Information Sharing

We do not sell personal information. We share information only with:

  • Service providers that help us operate the platform (including Twilio for SMS delivery, and hosting and analytics providers), bound by confidentiality obligations.
  • The Client that employs or engages the End User, for the purpose of timekeeping, payroll, and timesheet administration.
  • Legal authorities when required by law, subpoena, or court order.

5. Data Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect your information, including encryption in transit, access controls, and audit logging. No system is perfectly secure, but we take our obligations seriously and will notify Clients of any material security incident affecting their data as required by law.

6. Data Retention

We retain End User information for as long as the Client maintains an active account with us, and for a reasonable period thereafter to meet legal and tax recordkeeping obligations. Clients and End Users may request deletion of information by contacting us, subject to any overriding legal requirements.

7. Your Rights

Depending on your state or country of residence, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, port, or restrict the processing of your personal information, and to opt out of certain disclosures. U.S. residents in states such as California, Colorado, Virginia, Connecticut, and others with applicable privacy laws may exercise these rights by contacting us at the address below. We will not discriminate against you for exercising these rights.

8. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to individuals under 18, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date above reflects the latest revision. Material changes will be communicated to active Clients.
